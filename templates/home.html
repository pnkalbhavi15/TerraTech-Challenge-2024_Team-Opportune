Welcome!


A Business Correspondent (BC) is an individual or entity that acts as an intermediary between a bank or financial institution and its customers, particularly in rural or remote areas where traditional banking infrastructure may not reach. BCs play a crucial role in extending banking services to underserved and unbanked populations, promoting financial inclusion.

Role:
-Extending Banking Services: BCs are appointed by banks to provide banking services to customers in areas where setting up a brick-and-mortar branch is not feasible or cost-effective.
-Point of Contact: They serve as a point of contact for customers to access basic banking services such as deposits, withdrawals, remittances, loan applications, insurance, and other financial products.
-Customer Identification: BCs also help in customer identification and verification processes, which are essential for Know Your Customer (KYC) compliance.
-Technology Use: They often use technology like mobile phones, handheld devices, or other means to conduct transactions on behalf of the bank.
